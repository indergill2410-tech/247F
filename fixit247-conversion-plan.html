Fundamental Diagnosis

What's broken before we talk about fixes

Core Structural Problem

Fixit 24/7 is a two-sided marketplace with a one-sided revenue model. Tradies pay (credits + packs). Homeowners are free — with a single upsell path to a $49/month emergency subscription that requires enormous trust to purchase. This creates compounding revenue fragility: if tradie credit revenue softens, there is almost no homeowner revenue to buffer it.

Problem Current State Conversion Impact
Dual-audience identity crisis Landing page is 95% homeowner. Tradies get a thin nudge bar halfway down. Tradie bounce rate high
Emergency membership trust bomb 12-month commitment + $200 cancellation fee buried in fine print Kills purchase intent at decision moment
Credit cost opacity "30 to 800 credits" — 27× range shown to tradies before claiming Paralysis on every claim decision
Zero social proof near CTA Hero CTA has no adjacent star rating or job count Hesitation at peak intent
Wrong page section order Plus upsell appears before How It Works — upselling before explaining Confusion > mistrust > exit
Dual signup path dead-end Wrong role chosen = full re-registration required Silent drop-off in funnel
72-hour waiting period Emergency member pays $49 and can't use coverage for 3 days Immediate buyer's remorse
Thin homeowner monetisation One product: emergency membership. No mid-funnel upsells. Low homeowner LTV

Rebuilt User Funnels

Every step from stranger to paying customer

Homeowner Conversion Funnel

AWARENESS "I need a tradie" ↓ MICRO-COMMIT "What broke?" → picks category (no login required) ↓ PEEK VALUE "Here are 3 verified plumbers in your suburb" (gated behind email only) ↓ REGISTER Name + email + phone. No password yet — magic link sent. ↓ POST JOB Pre-filled from category selection. Only 2 additional fields. ↓ SOCIAL PROOF "2 tradies have seen your job" (real-time — drives return visit) ↓ CONVERSION They hire → job complete → trigger Plus upsell at peak satisfaction ↓ RETENTION "3 months later: your hot water system is 8 years old. Health check?"

Key Insight

The current flow asks for full registration before showing any value. Every high-converting marketplace (Amazon, Airbnb, Uber) does the opposite. Show value first — ask for commitment second.

Tradie Conversion Funnel

You missed them." ↓ FREE ENTRY "Sign up free — 1,111 credits waiting for you" (gift framing, not feature) ↓ PROFILE BUILD Progressive — 3 fields only to start. Rest unlocks after first login. ↓ FIRST CLAIM "Here's a nearby job — 45 credits. Most tradies win 1 in 3 claims." ↓ WIN TRIGGER They win → instant "You won! Buy credits to stay active" push notification ↓ RETENTION Monthly: "Your 1,111 credits just landed — 14 jobs in your area this week" ↓ UPSELL "You ran out of credits 3×. Pro subscribers never go dry — upgrade."

Critical Friction Fixes

The exact points where customers are dropping off right now

Fix 1 — Emergency Membership: The Trust Bomb

The 12-month commitment + $200 early cancellation fee appears after the user has decided to buy. In psychology, this is a "pattern interrupt" — a sudden jolt of doubt injected at peak purchase intent. This is your single biggest conversion blocker on the emergency page.

Current CopyPsychological ProblemReframe
"A$49/month"Monthly feels like a large recurring cost"Less than $1.65/day"
"12-month commitment"Sounds like a trap"One year of complete peace of mind"
"$200 early cancellation fee"Triggers loss aversion against purchaseRemove fee entirely, or rename "Pause fee" and move below fold
"72-hour waiting period"Buyer's remorse — paid and can't use"Activates fully in 72 hrs — add your home details now"
"2 callouts per year"Sounds limited"2 full emergency callouts (valued at $600+)"
"Fair use applies"Vague, feels like a gotchaReplace with specific: "1hr labour + parts up to $300/callout"

Highest Impact Option

Offer a $1 first-month trial. Conversions will increase dramatically. Your real LTV comes from month 7+ — the $200 cancellation fee recovers less revenue than the trials it kills.

Fix 2 — Credit Cost Opacity

Tradies are told claims cost "30 to 800 credits." That is a 27× range. A tradie who doesn't know if a job costs 30 or 800 credits will hesitate on every single claim. Hesitation on every claim compounds into much lower overall platform activity.

Fix Required

Show the exact credit cost on each job card before the tradie clicks to claim. "This job: 120 credits · Your balance: 890 credits · Est. value: $280–$450." Specificity kills anxiety. One change, measurable within a week.

Fix 3 — Wrong Role = Full Re-registration

If a tradie accidentally signs up as a homeowner (easy to do on a phone), they must completely re-register. This is a silent drop-off event — you'll see it as "registered but never claimed a job" in your analytics. It also creates duplicate accounts that pollute your data.

Fix: Add a role switch inside onboarding. "Actually, I'm a tradie/homeowner" — changes the profile type without forcing re-registration or losing data.

Fix 4 — Navbar Clutter

Current ItemProblemAction
Back/Forward browser buttonsRedundant — browser already has theseRemove entirely
"Fixit 24/7 Plus" as nav itemConfuses new visitors who don't know the productReplace with "Emergency Cover"
5 public nav linksHick's Law — too many choices slows decisionsReduce to: How it works · For tradies · Sign in · [Post a job]

Fix 5 — Mobile CTA Gap

On mobile, the primary CTA scrolls out of view almost immediately. There is no persistent conversion mechanism for mobile users who are reading but not yet ready to commit.

Fix: Add a sticky bottom CTA bar on mobile that appears after 30% scroll depth. "Post a job — free" in yellow. Disappears once the user scrolls to the footer. One-line implementation.

Priority Job Boost — A$9.99 per job

Homeowners pay $9.99 to have their job shown as "Priority" — pushed to the top of the tradie job feed with instant push notifications to all matching tradies in the area.

Why it converts: Low-friction one-off payment offered at job posting confirmation (not at registration). Homeowners with a genuine emergency (burst pipe, no hot water) will pay $9.99 without hesitation. Perfect upsell at the moment of highest stress.

Tradie Subscription Tiers — A$29 / A$79 per month

The "credits or nothing" model creates anxiety on every claim decision. Subscription tiers remove that anxiety at the top end and provide predictable revenue for the platform.

TierPriceCreditsExtra BenefitsPsychology
Free$01,111/monthBasic profileBroad funnel top
Pro$29/mo2,222/monthFeatured badge, analytics, response time stats"I'm serious about this"
Elite$79/moUnlimited claimsTop search placement, priority job alerts, verified badge, dedicated supportRemoves per-claim anxiety entirely

The Elite tier psychological win: A tradie paying $79/month with unlimited claims will claim more jobs, win more work, earn more, and become your most loyal user. Predictable cost = no anxiety = higher activity = better marketplace liquidity.

Job Protection Guarantee — A$15 per job

Platform-backed: if the tradie's work is defective within 30 days, Fixit covers a return visit or partial refund (capped at A$300).

Why it converts: Removes the #1 homeowner objection ("what if they do a bad job?"). Also generates quality data on every tradie that feeds your ranking algorithm. Tradies who consistently trigger claims get ranked lower — creating an automatic quality flywheel.

Implementation Roadmap

4 phases, sequenced by impact vs effort

Phase 1

Conversion Quick Wins — Pure Frontend

Weeks 1–2
  • Reorder landing page sections — move Plus teaser from position 3 to position 8
  • Add live job ticker to hero (hardcoded rotating examples first, live data later)
  • Simplify navbar: remove back/forward buttons, reduce to 3 links + 2 actions
  • Add social proof micro-strip to hero: ★★★★★ 4.9 · 3,200 jobs this month
  • Reframe emergency membership copy on landing and emergency pages (per-day pricing, remove fee language above fold)
  • Show exact credit cost on each job card before claiming
  • Add sticky mobile CTA bar (appears after 30% scroll)
Phase 2

Funnel Architecture Rebuild

Weeks 2–4
  • "What broke?" dropdown as the first hero interaction — micro-commit before suburb input
  • Peek value: show 3 tradies in suburb before requiring registration (email gate only)
  • Add stats strip (3 numbers: active tradies, jobs this month, avg response time)
  • Add role switch inside onboarding — no full re-registration required
  • Move top 3 FAQs to position 5 on landing page (after categories)
  • Tradie secondary CTA as ghost button in hero (not a separate bar)
Phase 3

New Monetisation Streams

Weeks 4–8
  • Priority Job Boost ($9.99) — upsell at job posting confirmation screen
  • Tradie Pro ($29/mo) and Elite ($79/mo) subscription tiers — Stripe + database
  • Job Protection Guarantee ($15/job) — offered at claim confirmation for homeowners
  • Win/loss analytics dashboard for tradies — drives voluntary upgrade to Pro
  • Referral programme — post-review trigger for homeowners, post-purchase trigger for tradies
Phase 4

Retention & Lifecycle Automation

Weeks 8–14
  • Homeowner NPS flow triggered 24hrs after job marked complete
  • Tradie monthly analytics email ("your win rate this month vs. area average")
  • Seasonal re-engagement email campaigns (HVAC before winter, etc.)
  • Credit depletion alert system (under 200 credits → alert → upsell to Pro)
  • Emergency Plus upsell triggered after homeowner's 2nd job posted
  • Background check badge product ($39 one-time) — verifiable trust signal
  • Promoted profiles ($49/week) — self-serve ad unit in category pages

The Single Highest-ROI Action

If you implement only one thing from this document, implement Phase 1 in full. The landing page reorder, hero social proof, and emergency copy reframe require zero backend changes and zero new features. They are pure copy and layout improvements that directly address the 8 core problems identified in the diagnosis. Conservative estimate: +40–60% increase in emergency membership conversion rate from copy changes alone.
