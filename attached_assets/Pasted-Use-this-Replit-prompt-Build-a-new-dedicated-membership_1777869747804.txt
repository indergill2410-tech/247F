## New dedicated page

Create a new page called:

**Fixit 24/7 Emergency**

This page should explain the membership in a highly persuasive and emotionally intelligent way. It should feel like a premium subscription landing page, not a boring insurance document.

The page should include these sections:

### 1. Hero
A premium landing-page hero repeating the $49/month offer and the emotional value proposition.

### 2. Why people join
This section should emotionally connect with real pain points:
- panic when something breaks
- fear of big surprise costs
- stress of searching for help in the middle of an emergency
- wanting to protect family, home, and routine
- wanting one reliable backup plan

Use emotionally resonant copy like:
- “When things go wrong, the stress hits fast.”
- “The real cost of emergencies is not just money — it’s panic, disruption, and feeling stuck.”
- “Fixit 24/7 Emergency helps you stay calm, act faster, and feel more in control.”

Emotional marketing and conversion copy work best when they connect the product to fear reduction, relief, trust, and control rather than only listing features. [thrillxdesign](https://thrillxdesign.com/emotional-triggers-in-copywriting-that-drive-signups/)

### 3. What’s included
Explain:
- emergency plumbing support
- electrical emergencies
- locksmith / lockout emergencies
- gas / hot water / roof leak emergencies
- car breakdown assistance support
- up to $2,000 covered value per membership year across eligible events

### 4. Why it’s worth it
This section should explain that:
- a single emergency can cost hundreds
- breakdowns and urgent repairs often come at the worst time
- for $49/month, members get year-round reassurance
- the offer is a fraction of what one major incident can cost

Use psychologically persuasive lines such as:
- “You hope you never need it. You’ll be relieved it’s there when you do.”
- “One membership can protect you from some of life’s worst-timed costs.”
- “You’re not just paying for assistance — you’re paying for fewer panic moments.”

Loss aversion, clarity, and emotional safety are strong conversion drivers when used carefully and authentically. [leadpages](https://www.leadpages.com/blog/psychology-of-conversion)

### 5. How it works
A simple step-by-step flow:
1. Join for $49/month
2. Get cover for eligible emergencies
3. Request help when something goes wrong
4. We organise support fast
5. Extra work outside covered value is quoted clearly

### 6. Peace-of-mind visuals
Use strong visual storytelling with premium, emotionally relevant images, not placeholders.

Add images that communicate:
- family at home feeling safe
- a stressed emergency moment turned calm
- a locked-out or broken-down scenario resolved
- tradie or breakdown assistance arriving
- home + vehicle support in one membership

Use visuals to reinforce calm, relief, reassurance, and preparedness. High-quality visuals help users understand the offer and feel the emotional outcome, which can improve conversions on landing pages.
