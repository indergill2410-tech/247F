01 of 06
Landing Page — Hero
Every visitor sees the hero. Outcome-focused copy + urgency outperforms range/catalogue framing.
Hero Headline
Before
"From a leaky tap to a full reno."
After
"Get a verified tradie to your door today."
Describes range → triggers outcome + urgency. "Today" activates time pressure without alarm.
Hero Subheading
Before
"Plumbing, electrical, lockouts, HVAC, carpentry and more. Fixit 24/7 connects homeowners with verified local tradies — for any job, any time."
After
"Post any job free in 2 minutes. Trusted tradespeople across Australia respond fast — plumbing, electrical, locksmith, HVAC and more."
Lead with the action (post free), not the product description.
Homeowner CTA Card
Before
Label: "I need a tradie"
Sub: "Post any job free — get quotes in minutes"
After
Label: "Fix it today"
Sub: "Free to post · Tradies respond in minutes"
Action-oriented label. Drops self-referential "I need a tradie" framing.
Tradie CTA Card
Before
Label: "I'm a tradie"
Sub: "$111 in free credits when you join"
After
Label: "Grow your trade business"
Sub: "$111/month free leads · No commissions"
Benefit-led, not identity-led. Removes "I'm a tradie" self-referential frame.
⚡ Mobile fix needed: Live Activity Ticker is currently hidden lg:flex — make it visible on mobile. It's the strongest social proof element on the highest-traffic device.
02 of 06
Register Page — Headlines, CTAs & Reassurance
The registration form is the primary conversion action. Every word matters at this step.
Step 1 Headline — Homeowner
Before
"Create account"
After
"Post your first job free"
Generic → action-oriented. User already chose homeowner role, reinforce that decision.
Step 1 Headline — Tradie
Before
"Create account"
After
"Claim your $111 in free leads"
The $111 is the #1 reason tradies sign up. Put it in the headline.
Submit Button — Tradie
Before
"Continue →"
After
"Claim my free credits →"
Verb-noun that reflects the reward. Reduces perceived risk at the click moment.
Reassurance Strip
Before
"🔒 No credit card  ·  ⏱ 30 seconds  ·  Cancel anytime"
After
"🔒 Secure  ·  ⚡ 30 seconds  ·  Free forever for homeowners"
"Cancel anytime" implies a subscription/commitment that doesn't exist for homeowners — it plants the idea of cancelling in a user's head right before they convert.
Tradie Gift Banner
Before
"Each claim costs from $22 — that's up to 5 free leads today. No credit card. No commission. Ever."
After
"That's up to 5 job leads this week, at zero cost. No credit card. No commission. No lock-in."
Move cost ($22) OUT of the benefit banner. Mentioning a cost mid-reward kills momentum.
Final Submit Buttons (Step 2)
Before
"Continue" (homeowner)    "Continue" (tradie)
After
"Create account & post my job →" (homeowner)     "Join free & get $111 now →" (tradie)
The submit button is the last thing a user reads before they convert. Make it a mini-promise, not a generic action word.
03 of 06
For Tradies Page — Hero Copy & CTA
Tradies respond to jobs and dollars — not "brand support, systems, and technology."
Hero Subheading
Before
"Partner with Fixit 24/7 to scale your trade business with brand support, systems, and technology. Stop relying only on expensive ads — build a scalable customer pipeline today."
After
"Get paying jobs in your area without spending on ads. Join free, claim local jobs with your monthly $111 credit, and build a pipeline of repeat customers — all without commission."
"Brand support, systems, technology" = abstract. "Scalable pipeline" = jargon. Rewrite in tradie language: jobs + dollars + no commission.
Primary CTA Button
Before
"Become a Trade Partner"
After
"Get my free $111 and start →"
"Trade Partner" = corporate. Tradies want jobs, not partnerships. Lead with the concrete reward.
Credit Card — Social Proof Addition
Before
"AUD free — every month" (static, no timing)
After
"AUD free — every month
✓ Credited to your wallet within 60 seconds of joining"
60-second certainty reduces "will this actually work?" doubt. Concrete timing = credibility.
How It Works — Step Copy
Before
Step 2: "Verified local tradies with the right skills and availability see your job. You'll hear back fast."

Step 4: "Accept only the jobs that suit your schedule. You're in full control of your workload."
After
Step 2: "Tradies respond — usually within 20 minutes. Emergency jobs jump to the top of every tradie's feed."

Step 4: "Claim only the jobs that fit your day. No obligation, no penalty — your schedule, your call."
Add the 20-minute number — specific and credible. Remove "full control" cliché, replace with concrete mechanic (no penalty).

Testimonial Cards — Outcome Badges
Before
Sarah M.: "Had a burst pipe at 11pm... Absolute lifesaver." — no outcome tag

Jake T.: "Been using it for 4 months... Booked solid most weeks." — no outcome tag
After
Sarah M. → badge: ✓ Fixed same night
Jake T. → badge: ✓ Booked solid in 4 months
Priya K. → badge: ✓ Hired first tradie online, 5★ result
Specific outcome badges let a visitor scan and self-match ("that could be me") without reading the full quote.
Final CTA Headline
Before
"Ready to get started?"
After
"Your tradie is waiting."
Passive question → declarative statement. After 30s of reading, the user doesn't need to be asked if they're ready — they need to be told.
Landing Page Section Order
Before
Hero → How It Works → Categories → Testimonials → FAQ → Final CTA
After
Hero → Stats bar → How It Works → Testimonials → FAQ → Categories → Final CTA
Move FAQ immediately after testimonials. People who just read a testimonial have a specific objection forming — the FAQ converts that doubt before they bounce. Categories are for exploration, not conversion.
